SAVE AN ANGEL WEBSITE

Responsive Redesign for a Non-Profit Organization

THE PROBLEM

Save An Angel is a pet rescue organization that requires the help of the community to fuel its mission efforts.

There is a high interest in getting involved in different areas of the animal rescue mission, including volunteering, adopting, fostering, donating and supporting fundraising events, but users are less likely to help as the required effort increases or resource availability decreases.

Therefore, users need a way to give back with minimal effort and roadblocks.

STAKEHOLDER MEETING TAKEAWAYS

Their main market is women aged roughly 45-55 years, who are tech-savy enough to find their way around facebook.

Most people access the website through mobile. Their current site is outdated and not responsive to screen size.

Save An Angel’s advertisement is most successful when it pulls at the heartstrings. Imagery evoking compassion and empathy pulls the users in.

Most all of their funding comes from individual donations, and they rely on many volunteers to execute their mission.

USER JOURNEY

NAVIGATION CARD SORTING

Card sorting is a hands-on, collaborative exercise where we brainstormed how the navigation would be organized. This included using post-it notes that represented the main, secondary, and footer navigation links.

WIREFRAMING

Initial sketch designs for user interface layout and functionality.

USER TESTING AND DESIGN ITERATION

I used the initial sketches to design low-fidelity prototypes in Adobe XD, which were used in the first round of user testing.

Users were asked to give general feedback on navigation elements and the homepage layout, along with completing 2 tasks: donate money to the organization and research information on the adoption process.

After each round of testing, users provided valuable feedback, which was reflected in the next design rendition.

After multiple rounds of testing and iterating, the final, high-fidelity design was completed.

WHAT THE USER DOES

Scrolls through the page, avoiding overwhelming forms and data input fields

Feels the urge to volunteer after empathizing with all the animals in the photos

Enters their email, a simple process with no upfront commitments

LESSONS LEARNED

Always have a backup strategy. Our stakeholder became wrapped up in prior commitments, so for a portion of the research phase, we had to find other outlets to collect data.

Stay focused on the most prominent needs of the user. Our initial navigation setup had too many key features and links. It took refinement to ensure the user could find what they needed quickly and easily.

Using Agile PM methodology, the team had the ability to accomplish massive goals by dividing up responsibilities, providing constructive feedback, and executing demanding deadlines. The results speak for themselves.

FUTURE EXPANSION

Finish “Donation Impact” Page. Show all the good the donations are contributing too, and give donors individual feedback

Add "Operation Relocation" page. Save An Angel’s animal transport program is an important part of its mission. It deserves its own landing page.

Social Media integration. Most of Save An Angel’s advertising and interaction with the community happens through social media. The goal would be to direct more users from social media to the website. For example, making the home of events the website instead of social media.

Add a Shop for merchandise. This will offer another way to raise funds and get the word out about Save an Angel.

FITFRIENDS MOBILE APP

Fitness App Design for iOS Devices

THE PROBLEM

Users want to maintain a consistent workout schedule, but they lack motivation and free time due to their job and/or social life interfering.

Therefore users need a way to set up workouts with a partner in order to maintain motivation and consistency.

This will add competition and accountability without compromising social experiences.

SURVEY RESULTS AND TAKEAWAYS

Do You Enjoy Exercising with a Partner?
What Benefits are there for Working Out with Others?
What Prevents You from Working Out with Friends/Partners More?
Final Takeaways

INTERVIEW TAKEAWAYS

These common themes, pain points, and desires were derived from conducting 8 user interviews.

USER PERSONA

The User Persona below was developed by collecting insight from the survey results and user interviews.

SWOT ANALYSIS

Reflecting on the Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats of FitFriends' Biggest Competitor.

FEATURE PRIORITIZATION

Through research and user interviews, the below items became focal points of the app.

USER JOURNEY

WIREFRAMING

Initial sketch designs for user interface layout and functionality.

USER TESTING AND DESIGN ITERATION

Users were asked to give general feedback on the flow of different tasks I asked them to complete, including creating a profile, and setting up a new workout.

The users were given two different designs, and asked to pick out elements from each design that they felt worked best and provided the best experience.

Each round of testing provided valuable feedback, which was implemented in each design iteration, until the final, high-fidelity design was created

A-B TESTING: CREATE PROFILE - LOW-FI

Each process had specific differences to narrow down the user's needs and pain points

Users preferred the option to skip specific steps if they pleased

To allow this, the flow was broken down into multiple pages like version 2, rather than one single-scrolling page

A-B TESTING: SET UP WORKOUT - LOW-FI

Once again, each process had specific differences to narrow down the user's needs and pain points

On this task, users preferred to have all information on one page, making it easier to stay organized

Users mentioned how important the visual layout was in helping guide them through the process one section at a time

USER FLOWS: FINAL DESIGN

SET UP ACCOUNT

WHAT THE USER SEES:

An intriguing design to get the user excited about using the app

Clear indicators when an item is selected or a button is active

The option to skip specific steps and streamline the sign up process

WHAT THE USER DOES

Creates a profile specific to their fitness habits and goals

Provides useful information for the app to assist in setting up future workouts

Receives visual confirmation when their task is complete

CREATE NEW WORKOUT

WHAT THE USER SEES:

Lively animations to make the process clear and simple

Variety that allows the user to be specific while not overwhelming them with details

The option to coordinate a workout with a friend, the key selling point of the app

WHAT THE USER DOES

Creates a workout that fits within their busy schedule

Chooses from a variety of options and time slots, keeping the setup flexible

Receives confirmation that the workout has been sent to their friend, ensuring accountability

LESSONS LEARNED

As a project manager, each member of the group has a different approach and personality. It’s important to take the time to learn more about each member, and be empathetic with their thought process.

Always design and iterate based on what is discovered in the research, without making assumptions. Relating to the core user doesn’t qualify as an exception.

A-B testing is the best way to finding a solution to a specific question or problem.

FUTURE EXPANSION

Implement a heart-monitoring feature to offer better fitness tracking abilities.

Expand beyond partner workouts to offer larger groups to all sync into a scheduled workout.

Further explore the possibilities of creating workouts with new friends, similar to a dating app, but with like-minded people who share similar fitness goals.
